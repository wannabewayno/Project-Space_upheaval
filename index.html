form or function?

Generally speaking, for items of the same function, form wins out. When items have an unknown function, form wins out. Form also alludes to having good function.

My philosophy, in general; putting form over function. At the end of the day the product still has to function, what will make it stand out is how it's presented. Keep elements few and nessecary.

just like a good pizza; you don't need 17 toppings, just 4 good quality ones.

All of us, no matter how humble; have a natural tendency to 'shiny things'. Big things are all built from small things, so master the small things and the big things will naturaly fall in place.

These principles are at my core, bringing them to my personal design principles. I believe in building a clean interior and exterioir product. If there's cables, they're colour coded, crimped and sheathed. If it's code, it's readable and well commented. If you spend this much detail under the hood, the outside will be a reflection of this too.

The final product is not simply an end justified by any means. It's when the whole is worth more than the sum of it's parts.
